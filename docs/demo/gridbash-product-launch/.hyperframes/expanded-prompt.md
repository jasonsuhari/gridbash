# GridBash launch video — expanded production prompt

## Style block

Create a 45.16-second, 1920×1080 launch film using the tactile cut-paper system
from `design.md`: paper `#F3EDE1`, ink `#171817`, soft ink `#2B2C2A`, mint
`#78C9B6`, yellow `#E2C94C`, and highlight `#FFF9EE`. Use Anton for decisive
display statements and Cascadia Mono for exact operational facts. The visual
reference is a manually keyframed After Effects piece made from matte paper and
shallow clay cards, not a 3D product film.

## Rhythm

ASSEMBLE — HUMAN PUNCH — BUILD — PEEL/REVEAL — PROVE — BRANCH — RECEIPT — HOLD.

The opening and product reveal are the two peaks. Product proof breathes. The
worktree section rebuilds energy. The install command resolves cleanly.

## Global rules

- Keep the existing narration, word timings, music, and low SFX mix.
- Use original talking-head footage without matting or AI face treatment.
- Use exact product footage for every readable product interaction.
- Use generated video only for the approved paper/clay assembly plates.
- Every scene begins from a designed hero frame and has a tactile full-frame wipe
  into the next scene; no empty frames or generic crossfades.
- One ambient behavior per scene. Once a card lands, it becomes completely still.
- Captions are 2–4 word groups in Anton, paper fill plus an ink outline, without a
  background pill. Keep captions clear of Jason's face and exact product controls.

## Beat 1 — The impossible operator (0.00–6.32)

### Concept

The film opens on scattered paper fragments. They register into a monumental
`10`, then six dark terminal tiles snap into place. At the punchline, the numeral
becomes a paper wipe that reveals Jason in the real room.

### Choreography

- 0.18–3.66: use `hook-assemble-seedance-mini.mp4`, retimed so its endpoint lands
  before “you’re not going to make it.”
- Keep the camera locked; overlay exact `10` or terminal details only if the
  generated plate drifts.
- 3.72–5.37: reveal `01-hook.mp4` full frame. A giant ink `MAKE IT.` stamps beside
  Jason, compresses once, then holds.
- 5.37–6.32: a bone-paper shutter closes and resets the canvas.

### Depth

Paper grain behind; numeral and terminal tiles midground; mint route cord and
caption foreground.

### Transition

Oversized bone sheet wipes right-to-left, 0.40 seconds, `power4.inOut`.

## Beat 2 — Controlled chaos (6.32–13.75)

### Concept

Jason stays human and warm while the workload physically accumulates around him.
Terminal cards arrive like cut pieces on an editor's table, then snap into a tidy
2×3 register on “each one.”

### Choreography

- `02-motivation.mp4` fills an asymmetrical rounded frame on the left.
- `TOKEN MAXING.` occupies the right half in two huge Anton lines.
- Six dark cards enter from alternating frame edges with decreasing overshoot.
- Mint cord routes through the cards only after the final card lands.
- The six cards snap into the 2×3 silhouette at 12.74 and become still.

### Transition

The registered card grid expands into six full-frame paper panels, revealing the
product plate beneath in 0.64 seconds.

## Beat 3 — Product reveal (13.75–20.04)

### Concept

Layered mint, bone, and yellow sheets peel open to expose one exact GridBash
window and the statement `ONE WINDOW. SIX AGENTS.`. The generated motion hands
off to live product footage without changing visual scale.

### Choreography

- 13.75–18.75: use `product-reveal-seedance-mini.mp4` as the tactile reveal.
- Overlay the exact product poster and exact HTML headline at the final register
  if the generated UI or letters soften.
- At 18.75, the product plate grows square-on into `product-demo.mp4`.
- No talking-head inset competes with the product hero frame.

### Transition

The UI plate scales from framed to full-screen while a mint slab moves behind it,
0.44 seconds, `expo.inOut`.

## Beat 4 — Real routing proof (20.04–29.75)

### Concept

The real GridBash capture is the entire visual argument. Tactile tabs behave like
editor annotations: they point, label, then get out of the way.

### Choreography

- Show `product-demo.mp4` from media offset 7.0, square-on and readable.
- On “every pane,” pop six tiny mint corner tabs in sequence.
- On “focus,” crop toward the selected real pane with no perspective distortion.
- On “select,” mark panes 2, 3, 5, and 6 with yellow paper tabs.
- On “broadcast,” draw one mint route stroke and let the four real replies land.
- Never imply inter-agent coordination; two panes visibly remain idle.

### Transition

A yellow tab grows into a full-width paper strip and pulls the next scene upward,
0.36 seconds, `power4.inOut`.

## Beat 5 — Worktree isolation (29.75–37.18)

### Concept

The grid becomes six isolated lanes made from cut strips. Jason explains the
benefit while the product and branch labels remain exact.

### Choreography

- Put `06-worktrees.mp4` in a rounded frame on the left.
- Put the real product poster on the right as a matte paper card.
- Six mint route strips peel from the product card; label them with the real
  `launch-main-01…06` and `launch/main-pane-01…06` names.
- Stamp `NO FILE FIGHTS.` in ink at the final phrase, one impact only.

### Transition

The six strips collapse into a single browser-shaped sheet that flips to the
GitHub screenshot, 0.44 seconds, `power4.inOut`.

## Beat 6 — Receipt and install (37.18–45.16)

### Concept

End with proof, not another abstract claim. The live GitHub page sits on the
paper table like a printed receipt; then the install command becomes the only
remaining object.

### Choreography

- Recapture the repository immediately before rendering and show the real live
  star count, MIT license, and repository URL.
- The platform packages are not live. Use `assets/voice-truth-safe.wav`, omit the
  spoken OS clause and all cross-platform tabs, and show only `OPEN SOURCE · MIT`.
- The truth-safe edit shifts “You can install it…” from 42.20 to 40.16. At that
  point, a bone sheet covers the GitHub card and reveals the install plate.
- `npm install -g gridbash` types on as exact HTML, then holds through the end.

### Transition

Final color dip to ink over 0.70 seconds after the command has held long enough.

## Recurring motifs

- Mint cord means routing or branching—never decoration.
- Yellow tabs mean selection or proof.
- Bone paper wipes mark topic changes.
- Dark terminal cards remain rigid after landing.

## Negative prompt

No chrome, black glass, neon, HUD graphics, particles, glossy 3D, camera orbit,
fake depth-of-field, gradient text, pill captions, fake terminal copy, invented
GitHub facts, tiny web typography, equal card grids, or residual wobble.
