# GridBash Launch Taste Laws

## Standing laws

1. Measure style with timings, states, geometry, and sound events; never rely on adjectives alone.
2. Give every scene and overlay a narrative job. Motivate it or delete it.
3. Use real GridBash footage and exact product wording. Do not invent capabilities, users, or metrics.
4. Treat Jason’s real desk as an operator station, not as a cutout over a synthetic AI background.
5. Keep settled UI and labels completely still unless continued motion communicates routing or activity.
6. Make every riser or whoosh land on a visual impact or deliberate release.
7. Use clicks for selection, routed ticks for pane targeting, and one reserved impact family for product/CTA reveals.
8. Reserve the largest visual and sonic impact for the GridBash/product reveal, not the opening joke.
9. Review the motion with dialogue and music; stills cannot approve timing.
10. Keep captions readable on mute without covering Jason’s face or the product proof.
11. Preserve product footage legibility; no perspective tilt, fake device frame, or decorative blur over important UI.
12. Use green for selection/routing, blue for focus, and amber for completion only.

## Learned corrections

| Observed failure | Measurable cause | Standing rule | Verification |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Existing launch teaser feels informational but silent | no music or semantic sound system | lock a licensed soundtrack and map scene impacts before animation | compare scene boundaries with the music map |
| Camera source risks looking soft in a 1080p master | source is 640x360, a 3x linear upscale | use the camera as an intentional plate, control its displayed size, and add only restrained sharpening | inspect exported 1080p face detail at 100% |
| “Cross-platform” could outrun the release | public v0.1.6 package is Windows x64 | require Windows, Linux, and macOS release artifacts before publication | query npm and GitHub release assets on ship day |
| Product language can drift into generic orchestration claims | GridBash routes independent CLI sessions; it is not an agent framework | show routing and worktree proof instead of claiming agents coordinate themselves | compare copy with README and source behavior |
| Active caption words read as fused phrases | 1.07 scale consumed the narrow inter-word margin | keep a minimum 0.22em word margin and cap active-word scale at 1.04 | inspect captions at hook, reveal, and worktree timecodes |
| Punchline stamps can compete with dialogue captions | both elements occupied the bottom 180px safe area | keep narrative stamps above the persistent caption lane | inspect the worktree scene at 32.5s |

## Explicit preferences

- Keep: Jason’s changed lines, token-maxing language, real desk, real product footage, dense operational typography.
- Avoid: neon cyberpunk, purple gradients, fake dashboards, glowing HUD rings, tiny labels, generic corporate stock music.
- Approved direction: “Close Up” rhythmic underscore and the desk-as-operator-station visual treatment.
