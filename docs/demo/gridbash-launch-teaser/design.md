# GridBash Launch Teaser Design

## Visual world

An operator dispatch board for coding agents: precise, dense, and practical. The
terminal grid is the hero, surrounded by the visual language of a control room:
status rails, routing labels, registration marks, and terse operational copy.

## Palette

- Canvas: `#0b1118`
- Panel: `#121720`
- Foreground: `#f0f4f9`
- Muted copy: `#8fa2b7`
- GridBash green: `#29d392`
- Focus blue: `#48a5ff`
- Completion amber: `#ffca5c`

## Typography

- Display voice: `Archivo Black`, 400. Broad, compressed statements that feel
  stamped onto an operations board.
- Operational voice: `Cascadia Mono`, 400 and 700. Commands, agent names,
  counters, labels, and metadata.
- Display tracking: `-0.045em`.
- Operational tracking: `0.02em` to `0.09em` for labels.

## Shape and depth

- Corners are compact: 8px for labels, 18px for terminal hardware.
- Borders are structural and visible at video scale: 2px.
- Depth comes from a terminal-window shadow, layered rules, and controlled
  parallax. No floating glass cards.

## Motion language

- Primary transition: directional push with velocity-matched blur.
- Accent transition: zoom-through into the install command.
- Motion verbs: stamp, route, draw, lock, push, and resolve.
- Ambient motion is restrained to one mechanism per beat.

## Do

- Keep real product footage legible and dominant during the proof beat.
- Use green for selection/routing, blue for focus, and amber for completion.
- Anchor typography to edges and let rules create paths through the frame.
- Preserve enough hold time to read every claim without audio.

## Do not

- Do not use cyan-on-purple gradients, gradient text, glowing HUD rings, or
  particle filler.
- Do not invent fake product capabilities or fake user metrics.
- Do not center equal-weight stacks except for the final install command.
- Do not use Inter, Roboto, Syne, or generic dashboard cards.
