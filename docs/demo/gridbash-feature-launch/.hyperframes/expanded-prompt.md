# Expanded Production Prompt — GridBash Feature Launch

## Title and style block

Create a 96-second, 1920×1080 HyperFrames product-launch film titled **THE GRID
WAS ONLY THE BEGINNING**. The visual world is the GridBash operations field
manual described in `design.md`: warm tactile cut paper on `#F3EDE1`, ink
`#171817`, soft ink `#2B2C2A`, GridBash mint `#78C9B6`, tab yellow `#E2C94C`,
and paper highlight `#FFF9EE`. Display copy uses Anton 400; operational copy
uses Geist Mono 400/700. The mood is controlled chaos becoming deliberate
coordination: energetic, hand-directed, physical, and exact.

The film is text-led and must work muted. Real GridBash footage is the visual
receipt. Product schematics quote only current repository facts and remain
clearly art-directed callouts rather than simulated screenshots.

## Rhythm declaration

`HOOK — proof — BUILD — fast-fast — BREATHE — ORCHESTRATE — RESOLVE — CTA`

- 0–6s: one hard hook with a held final phrase.
- 6–16s: real product proof and a quick recap of the first film.
- 16–50s: three dense feature chapters with different spatial rhythms.
- 50–62s: profile/auth breath with deliberate reading time.
- 62–76s: orchestration peak and the most active route choreography.
- 76–86s: resilience resolve; calmer and heavier.
- 86–96s: cross-platform stamp and install CTA hold.

## Global rules

- Build every scene as a strong static hero frame before attaching motion.
- Every scene has 8–10 visible elements across background, middle, and
  foreground layers, including at least two structural/decorative elements.
- Every scene has two focal points: a decisive Anton claim and either real
  product proof or a large operational schematic.
- Use one ambient timeline motion per scene: paper-tab breath, route drift,
  slow proof push, or subtle rule pulse. No infinite repeats.
- Primary scene handoff is a pair of full-frame paper cover panels, 0.46s,
  `power4.inOut`. Use the vertical page push for scenes 3→4 and 6→7. Use an ink
  dip at the final scene only.
- Outgoing content stays fully visible until the cover transition begins. No
  scene content exits before its transition.
- Entrances begin 0.18–0.26s after scene activation and combine at least three
  directions/eases. Use explicit `fromTo` states for deterministic seeking.
- Real footage stays square-on in a thick ink frame; a child image/video may
  receive a 1→1.025 proof push while its wrapper handles the entrance.
- All claims are grounded in `README.md`, `docs/REFERENCE.md`,
  `docs/releases/v0.2.0.md`, and the current UI shortcut strings.

## Scene 1 — The grid was only the beginning (0.00–6.00)

### Concept

The cutting table looks almost empty until a huge mint 2×3 grid stencil snaps
into register. A yellow divider tab cuts across it and reveals the phrase **THE
GRID WAS ONLY THE BEGINNING** in three timed statements. The viewer should feel
the old launch film being physically opened into a much larger manual.

### Mood direction

After Effects title design made from thick paper; confident editorial poster,
not nostalgic scrapbook.

### Depth layers

- BG: paper fiber; oversized ghost `0.2` release mark; two cropped registration
  crosses; a slow mint route segment.
- MG: monumental grid stencil; Anton statement in three block spans.
- FG: yellow `FIELD MANUAL / 09 CHAPTERS` tab; thin ink rule; issue-style
  `CURRENT PRODUCT / JUL 2026` stamp.

### Animation choreography

- Grid stencil STAMPS from 0.82 scale with a short back overshoot.
- First phrase CUTS in from the left; second DROPS from the top; final phrase
  PUNCHES from scale 0.72.
- Yellow tab SLIDES from the right and the rule DRAWS outward.
- Registration crosses SNAP in on alternating axes.
- Route segment drifts once during the reading hold.

### Transition out

Two-block paper cover from right to left, 0.46s, `power4.inOut`; the mint block
leads and the paper-highlight block trails by 0.06s.

### Transition out

Primary two-block paper cover, left to right, 0.46s.

## Scene 3 — One workspace, many shapes (16.00–27.00)

### Concept

The field manual becomes a physical workspace map. Three tabbed grids fan out
like indexed sheets; one tab comes forward, a 3×3 grid folds into a 2×3 layout,
then a single focused pane expands to fill its grid area. The claim is
**TAB IT. RESIZE IT. ZOOM IN.**

### Mood direction

Precision paper engineering: architectural fold diagrams with lively snap and
no fake 3D camera.

### Depth layers

- BG: paper fiber; ghost tab labels; vertical mint datum line; cropped row and
  column numbers.
- MG: three offset grid sheets; large 3×3→2×3 resizer schematic; full-area
  focused-pane plate.
- FG: `ALT+N / ALT+T` tab; `ALT+L` fold label; `ALT+F` zoom stamp; swap arrows.

### Animation choreography

- Grid sheets FAN from different axes with distinct rotation settles.
- Active tab SLIDES forward; inactive tabs SETTLE behind it.
- Right column FOLDS away to demonstrate resize.
- Summary labels in the retained cells REGISTER after the fold.
- Focused pane EXPANDS with `expo.inOut`; zoom stamp lands after expansion.
- Swap arrows CROSS once and stop.

### Transition out

Accent vertical page push upward, 0.50s, `power3.inOut`.

- FG: `QUIET` badge; `DONE` badge; `ALT+P` summary tab; mouse-copy bracket.

### Animation choreography

- Headline CRASHES from the left and settles hard.
- Pane cards CASCADE from alternating horizontal directions.
- Summary strips TYPE ON in short chunks, then hold.
- Quiet badge FLIPS into place; done badge STAMPS separately.
- Scrollback strip ROLLS upward once.
- Previous-pane receipt PULLS from behind the contact sheet.

### Transition out

Primary two-block paper cover, right to left, 0.46s.

### Mood direction

- MG: full-width command bar; dictation waveform and unsubmitted transcript;

- FG: `ALT+C`, `ALT+SHIFT+V`, rename, swap, sleep/wake, restart, copy, and image

### Animation choreography

- Transcript TYPES ON while waveform DRAWS; an `ENTER NOT PRESSED` stamp lands.

- Sleeping pane DIMS to soft ink; hover tab WAKES it back to mint.
- Restart glyph ROTATES once, then stops.
- Copy bracket CLAMPS around a short terminal excerpt.

### Transition out

Primary two-block paper cover, left to right, 0.46s.

## Scene 7 — Give the grid a goal (62.00–76.00)

### Depth layers

- FG: awake/asleep toggles; validation stamp; `ALT+G / ALT+U` tab; status ticket.

- Status ticket slides into the bottom bar as the final peak detail.

### Transition out

Primary two-block paper cover with yellow lead panel, 0.46s.

### Mood direction

### Depth layers

- MG: three platform sheets; npm package sleeve; install command; real product
  poster.
- FG: `OPEN SOURCE / MIT` receipt; repository URL; yellow `STABLE` tab.

### Animation choreography

- Platform sheets FOLD in from three edges with different eases.
- Package sleeve SNAPS around them.
- Product poster REGISTERS from the right while the install command EXPANDS from

- `RUN THE WHOLE SQUAD` PUNCHES in two timed blocks.
- Open-source receipt and repository URL SLIDE into opposite corners.
- All motion stops for the final 2.2-second copyable hold.
- Ink dip fades the whole film at 95.28s over 0.72s.

### Transition out

Final ink-color dip to `#171817`, 0.72s, `power1.inOut`.

## Recurring motifs

- Mint route cord always means deliberate targeting.
- Yellow tabs identify direct controls, commands, or proof labels.
- Registration crosses mark a feature becoming available or resolved.
- Paper receipts carry factual product claims.
- A small `GB / 0.2` release mark advances clockwise around the frame between
  scenes, giving continuity without decorative particles.

## Negative prompt

No neon, cyan-on-dark, purple gradients, glass cards, HUD rings, particles,
gradient text, generic dashboards, identical feature tiles, tiny metadata,
floating centered stacks, fake readable terminal UI, fake metrics, synthetic
camera flights, wall-clock animation, jump cuts, premature exits, residual
wobble, or unsupported cross-platform/security claims.
